Indra, being thus disappointed of his
offerings, was exceedingly angry and thus
addresscd a cohort of his attendant clouds,
called Samvarttaka: ‘‘Ho, clouds’’ he said,
‘*hear my words and without delay execute
what I command. The insensate cowherd
Nanda, assisted by his fellows, has withheld
the usual offerings to us, relying upon the
protection of Krsna. Now, therefore, afflict the
cattle, that are their sustenance and whence
their occupation is derived, with rain and
wind. Mounted upon my elephant, as vast as a
mountain peak, I will give you aid in
strengthening the tempest.’’
